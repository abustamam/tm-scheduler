# Glossary of Toastmasters terms

New members hear a lot of unfamiliar words in their first few meetings. Here's a
plain-language guide to the ones that come up most often.

## Around the meeting

- **Agenda** — the running order for a meeting: who's speaking, evaluating, and taking each role.
- **Icebreaker** — your very first speech, a short introduction of yourself. Everyone starts here.
- **Table Topics** — the impromptu-speaking segment, where you answer a surprise question in a minute or two.
- **Word of the Day** — a word the Grammarian introduces for members to work into their speaking.
- **Business meeting** — a short session for club decisions, sometimes held before or after the regular program.

## Roles you'll hear named

- **Toastmaster of the Day** — the meeting's host and MC.
- **General Evaluator** — evaluates the meeting itself and leads the evaluation team.
- **Ah-Counter** — notes filler words to help members speak more cleanly.
- **Grammarian** — introduces the Word of the Day and notes memorable language.

## Program and structure

- **Pathways** — the modern Toastmasters learning experience, organized into paths, levels, and projects.
- **Base Camp** — the online platform where members access their Pathways materials and track progress.
- **DTM** — Distinguished Toastmaster, the program's highest education award.
- **District / Division / Area** — the layers of Toastmasters organization above your club, largest to smallest.
- **TI** — Toastmasters International, the worldwide nonprofit that supports all clubs.

Don't worry about memorizing these — you'll pick them up naturally, and any
member will happily explain a term you don't recognize.
