# Speech contests

A few times a year, clubs hold speech contests — friendly competitions that add
a bit of sparkle to the calendar and send winners on to compete at the area,
division, and district level. They're a great way to stretch yourself, and just
as fun to watch.

## Common contest types

- **International Speech** — 5–7 minute prepared speeches; the winner's path leads toward the World Championship of Public Speaking.
- **Evaluation** — contestants all evaluate the same test speaker, then deliver their evaluations one at a time.
- **Table Topics** — a single impromptu question, answered by each contestant in turn.
- **Humorous, Tall Tales, and other seasonal contests** — clubs and districts choose which to run.

## Who can compete

Eligibility rules vary a little by contest and are set by Toastmasters
International's official *Speech Contest Rulebook*, which is updated regularly.
In general you need to be a member in good standing of the club you represent,
and prepared speeches must be substantially your own original work. Always check
the current rulebook — or ask your contest chair — before you sign up.

## How judging works

A panel of trained judges scores contestants on content, delivery, and (for
prepared speeches) how well the speech is built. Timing matters too: go under or
over the allowed window and you can be disqualified, so know your times.

## Why enter

Win or lose, a contest is a deadline that makes you a sharper speaker — and clubs
love to cheer their members on.
