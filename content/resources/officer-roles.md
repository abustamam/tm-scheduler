# Club officer roles

A Toastmasters club is run by its members. Each year (or half-year, depending on
the club) members elect a team of officers who keep the club healthy and
welcoming. Serving as an officer is one of the best leadership-development
experiences the program offers — and every role is a chance to grow.

## The executive committee

- **President** — leads the club, chairs the executive committee, and makes sure every member gets a great experience.
- **Vice President Education** — the busiest officer: schedules speakers and roles, tracks members' Pathways progress, and keeps the meetings running well.
- **Vice President Membership** — welcomes guests, follows up with prospective members, and keeps the club growing.
- **Vice President Public Relations** — promotes the club to the wider community, online and off.
- **Secretary** — keeps the club's records, minutes, and correspondence.
- **Treasurer** — manages the club's finances and dues.
- **Sergeant at Arms** — sets up the room, manages equipment, and helps meetings start and end on time.

## The Club Success Plan

Strong clubs plan their year. Most officer teams set goals against the
Distinguished Club Program — a simple scorecard of membership, education, and
training milestones — so the whole club knows what "a great year" looks like and
can celebrate hitting it together.

## Getting involved

You don't have to wait to be asked. If a role interests you, tell your VP
Education or President — clubs are almost always glad to help a member step up.
